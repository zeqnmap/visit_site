Bot for Church of St. Nicholas the Wonderworker

Test Live Bot

Raised in an Orthodox Christian family, faith plays an important role in our lives. We might not make it to church every Sunday, but celebrating major Christian holidays is a non-negotiable tradition that keeps our family deeply connected especially with our grandparents. We always celebrate at the Church of St. Nicholas the Wonderworker in Minsk Region, because here a lot of our friends who have truly become like family to us.

One day, I realized, that some people couldn't attend church due to chronic illnesses or the vulnerabilities of old age. To support them, I developed a Telegram bot that allows homebound or elderly parishioners to submit prayer requests for healing and for the departed. Additionally, I made a guide for users who want to donate and support our local priests through the national payment system (ERIP).

Now, the bot supports over 70 parishioners who previously couldn't attend church's service and generating around 40 prayer requests weekly. During the winter, this number scales up significantly as mobility becomes more challenging in this area.

The bot is live on the church's official Telegram channel. Additionally, you may visit Archpriest Vladimir's Bashkirov website.

Channel Website

You can see how the church looks like. It's located at Ulitsa Dzerzhinskogo, Haradzišča, Minsk Region(53.830588, 27.337165). The church was recently reconstructed from wood to stone, making it truly cozy.

Upon launching the bot for the first time, users are greeted with a short description of its features.

After pressing /start command, the bot will send all available operations, including : submitting a prayer request, asking a question or donate through ERIP.

If users choose to submit a prayer request, they are presented with two options: the first for healing, and the second for the departed.

If users choose to make a donation, they are provided with a direct link or a step-by-step guide on how to complete the payment.
