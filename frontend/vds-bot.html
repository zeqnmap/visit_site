VDS Onboarding Bot

Test Live Bot

While I was completing my internship at the VDS factory as a laser machine operator, their HR department found out through my college that I had built the Telegram schedule bot for students. Recognizing my skills, the HR department asked me to build an onboarding bot for their new hires. They sent me to numerous offices where I listened to employees from different departments and noted down what features the bot should have. Just two hours later, I came back and we designed the architecture.

During the first week, I focused only on onboarding features for new employees, but during the second week, they gave me an additional task to add a document request function for all employees. After successfully completing my task, I was asked to build another bot for the factory, allowing shop foremen to report on the tasks performed by their workers. This bot was three times larger and more complex, the product faced some user adoption challenges, and the bot was discontinued.

I decided not to create an individual page for this bot because I believe that a project needs to be useful and bring an impact to people. Since this bot didn't, it holds little value for me as a product, but of course, I gained a lot of experience from it. The company paid me for both products, so I was absolutely delighted.

Since I was only 16, the company was required to transfer the money through my college, and only after that process was the money given to me. All students were shocked by how I got paid, but I only said that in my free time, I also worked as a developer.

Upon launching the bot for the first time, new employees are greeted with a motivational quote and clear guidance on their next steps.

By selecting the 'Socialization' button, employees are presented with a list of available options. Clicking on any option provides a detailed description of that topic.

By selecting the 'Transport' button, employees are presented with options on how to commute to work. The bot outlines two ways: corporate transportation or personal car. The corporate shuttles run along two different routes covering opposite areas of Minsk. Additionally, the bot provides the schedule for both routes with timetables for each stop. For those driving personal vehicles, the bot provides instructions on parking near the office entrances.

By clicking on 'Usual Questions', employees can find answers to basic questions covered by most popular topics such as payroll and meals. If employees have a more specific question, they can ask it using the 'Unusual Questions' button. Then users enter their name and phone number, so the administrator can answer their questions as soon as possible.

The final option is 'Document Request', available not only to new employees but to the rest of the staff as well. The request can include payslips, employment records, or vacation balance statements. Within three business days, the documents are ready for pickup at the HR desk.

For example, selecting 'Payslips' requires entering employee's full name and phone number for validation purposes and a quick follow-up in case of any issues.
